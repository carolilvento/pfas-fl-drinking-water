Minimizing PFAS Exposure: Protecting Your Health and Home

By Carolina Ilvento

The presence of PFAS (Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances) in our environment necessitates proactive measures to reduce exposure risks. Here are key steps to protect yourself and your family from PFAS:

Monitoring Your Drinking Water

  1. Testing Your Water: To check the presence of PFAS, particularly PFOA, PFOS, GenX chemicals, and other PFAS groups like PFBA and PFBS in your drinking water, consider using a Cyclopure PFAS kit for testing.
  2. Effective Filters: Invest in water filtration systems using granular activated carbon (GAC) or reverse osmosis (RO) filters. These can significantly reduce PFAS substances. Ensure the filter is certified to NSF/ANSI standards for PFAS removal.

Selecting Water Filters for PFAS Removal

When it comes to safeguarding your drinking water from PFAS, choosing the right water filter is fundamental. Some effective options include:

  • Reverse Osmosis (RO) Systems: RO filters utilize a semi-permeable membrane to remove a wide range of contaminants, including PFAS. These systems force water through the membrane, leaving impurities behind. RO filters can effectively reduce PFAS levels in your drinking water. Keep in mind that they may have a slower flow rate compared to standard faucets.
  • Granular Activated Carbon (GAC) Filters: GAC filters use porous carbon to adsorb contaminants from water. They can effectively remove PFAS substances, making them a solid choice for PFAS reduction. GAC filters might also have a lower water flow compared to regular faucets.

Both RO and GAC filters are effective at reducing PFAS in drinking water. Your choice may depend on your specific needs, such as the range of contaminants you want to address and your water flow preferences.

To ensure the effectiveness of any water filter in removing PFAS, make sure it is certified to meet NSF/ANSI standards for PFAS removal. Staying informed and making an informed choice will help you protect your household from PFAS exposure.

Avoiding Products Containing PFAS

In addition to safeguarding your drinking water, it's important to be mindful of products that may contain PFAS. Here are some strategies to help you avoid those:

  • Check Product Labels: Scrutinize product labels for ingredients that include terms like "fluoro" and "perfluoro." These chemicals can indicate the presence of PFAS. Avoid or minimize the use of products with these ingredients.
  • Non-Stick Cookware: Reduce your use of non-stick cookware, as it may have coatings containing PFAS. Instead, opt for cookware made of stainless steel or cast iron, which are PFAS-free alternatives.
  • Stain-Resistant Products: When selecting furniture and carpets, avoid items labeled as "stain-resistant." These products may have been treated with PFAS-based stain repellents. Opt for alternatives that do not carry this label.
  • Household Dust: Be aware that household dust can contain PFAS particles shed from items with PFAS content. To minimize exposure, vacuum your living spaces frequently to pick up dust and reduce its presence.
  • Cosmetic Products: Keep an eye on cosmetic products as well. Some cosmetics may contain PFAS ingredients, particularly those claiming long-lasting, waterproof, or smudge-proof properties. Check the product labels and ingredient lists when buying cosmetics and consider choosing PFAS-free alternatives.
  • Cleaning Products: Certain cleaning products, especially those marketed for their stain-resistant or water-repellent qualities, may contain PFAS. Opt for cleaning solutions that do not include PFAS in their formulations and labels.
  • Outdoor Gear: Some outdoor gear, like waterproof clothing, may have PFAS-based treatments. When shopping for outdoor apparel and gear, look for options that use alternative, non-PFAS waterproofing technologies.
  • Food Packaging: While the use of PFAS in food packaging has been reduced, it's still a good practice to minimize exposure by reducing the consumption of fast food, microwave popcorn, and takeout, which may be packaged in materials treated with PFAS. Choosing fresh, unprocessed foods can help lower your exposure.
  • Product Contact: If you have concerns about specific products in your home, consider reaching out to the Consumer Product Safety Commission's Consumer Ombudsman. They can provide guidance and information about PFAS in consumer products. Contact them at (301) 504-8120 or via email at ConsumerOmbudsman@cpsc.gov, or visit the CPSC's website for additional resources.
  • Fish Consumption: Be aware of fish consumption guidelines in your region, especially if your local waterways are affected by PFAS contamination.
  • Outdoor Activities: If you engage in activities in lakes, streams, or rivers, always wash your hands before eating if you've had contact with foam or water. Prevent accidental swallowing of water and ensure you, your family, and pets rinse or shower after such activities.
