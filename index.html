Unveiling PFAS in Florida's Drinking Water:

Is Your Water Safe?


By Carolina Ilvento

Are you concerned about the presence of "forever chemicals" in your drinking water sources?

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are notorious for their remarkable persistence in both the environment and the human body. The composition and bioaccumulation of these substances have been associated with a spectrum of health risks, including potential links to cancer, developmental issues, and decreased fertility. The most common route of exposure to PFAS is through the consumption of contaminated drinking water.

This project introduces a comprehensive overview of the chemicals along with an interactive map that enables you to explore water providers in your area and check for recent testing conducted at water treatment facilities and in individual households' tap water.
